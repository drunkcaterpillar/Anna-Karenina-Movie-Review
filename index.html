This website covers the ins and outs of me geeking out about one of my favourite period dramas of all time - Anna Karenina!

Anna Karenina, based on Tolstoy's novel, makes the audience relate to every single character at some point in their personal story, even to this date. That is pretty crazy.

Russian high society in the late 1800s. It is like a detailed study of every possible human emotion.

It connects with the viewers because of the flaws in the characters and their evolution over time because of the events surrounding their lives.

One can relate to the contrasts: city/country, married/single, rich/poor or man/woman.

It is noticeable how every character, even the ones who behaved poorly had some small redeeming quality.

It's a black and white story of love and adultery but with so many nuances that one realizes how hard it can be to judge people's actions.

Tolstoy was a genius, and so are the actors who portray the characters in the movie, especially Keira Knightley as the protagonist, Anna.

How many novels have been written about adultery? How many that truly understand a woman's point of view? How many that sympathetically present a heroine succumbing to temptation, showing the dilemma women face(d)?

Yes, Anna Karenina is long-winded.

Yes, it seems like a soap opera at times. But remove the context, considering only what is now a fairly familiar story of love and betrayal, it is not easy to miss the point. Also, much of the pathos and prose is brilliant.

It just gets deeper, better, richer.
